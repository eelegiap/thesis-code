Alignment Explorer
This visualization requires a word to be selected (clicked) in the texts. Once a word is selected, the alignment explorer displays three related concepts from left to right: 1) the number of times that the selected word is not translated as the immediate translation (the aligned translation), 2) the number of times that the selected word is translated as the immediate translation, and 3) the number of times that the immediate translation is not translated as the selected word.
Hovering over segments of the visualization will provide specific information on the frequencies of these phenomena.
Position(s) in Text
When you click on a word, this panel displays all the instances in which the word and its corresponding translation are used in the text as a whole. Dots more to the right represent instances of the word later in the text. The dots in blue correspond to the selected word in the source text, while the dots in red correspond to the selected word in the translation text.
This panel provides to the user data about word frequency and spread. Furthermore, this panel (in conjunction with the “Alignment Explorer”) helps users determine how often a selected word and its immediate translation appear together in the text.
Google N-Gram Viewer (Usage over time)
Mining a select corpus of texts, the Google Ngram Viewer displays the frequency with which a given word has been used from 1800 to the present. When you click on a word, WADL feeds the selected word and its corresponding immediate translation into Ngram, and displays a chart showing the frequencies of the words. The blue line is the frequency of the source document word, while the red line is the frequency of the translation document word. You can hover over the graph to judge the divergence between the frequency of the words at a given time. You can also select a line or label to gray out the other line. Our intention in providing this data is to assist translators and literary scholars in determining whether the selected word and its translation closely correspond.
